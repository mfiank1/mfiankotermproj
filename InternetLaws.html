What exactly are Internet Laws

         

Internet laws are legal laws, principles, and regulations that covers the general use of all the Internet. Internet legislation is covered by both the Federal and State governments. Different states have diffrent laws regarding aspects of the internet, for example there are about 47 states the have pretty much NO laws surronding internet privacy.

Examples of the many types of Internet Laws

  • Laws that govern online marketing such as:
    • Can-SPAM Act: regulates spam email, gives you the right to have to have spam emailers stop emailing you
  • Laws that govern intellectual propety
    • Copyright: Prevents unauthorized copying of peoples work
  • Laws that govern online privacy
    • Childrens Online Privacy Act: Allows parents to control what infomation can be collected from their children
     
               

    Who do US internet laws protect?

               

    The US's internet laws only protect those that are residents of the country, citizens who travel outside the US must abide bby the laws of the country they are currently in, such as a tourist visiting China must abide by their Laws
